How to use the CAB Stanza Viewer

The Stanza Viewer gives further information about the stanza, regarding not only the linguistic features, but also some other important data, such as the parallels or the ritual context.


The first box offers the text of the stanza and its division in smaller units (a, b, c, d, etc.)


If you want to navigate to the previous or the next stanza, press on the arrows on the left and right sides respectively.


Morphological Glossing: Morphological details and the possible meanings of the words in the stanza, all shown in parallel.

Pahlavi Version: a standardized Pahlavi transcription of the intended stanza is given on the basis on the four manuscripts 400. Significant variants from other manuscripts (410, 500, 510) are indicated in a reduced critical apparatus. It does not intend to be a critical edition, but an accompanying text that might contribute to the text critical analysis of the Avestan text.

Previous Editions: the version of the corresponding text in previous editions (Spiegel, Westergaard and Geldner) are displayed here, with their corresponding numbering.

Speaker: the instruction about the narrator(s) of this stanza is shown in this section and the sources available to know it, either manuscripts or bibliography. If there are discrepancies, these are shown. When there is no information about this specific stanza, it is given the number of the last previous stanza where there is available information. E.g. for Y1.2 vid. Y1.1.

Apparatus: we produce the apparatus with the tool Textual Communities. Each variant is analysed, verse by verse. If you want to open Textual Communities, click on See dynamic ... There you will find the variants already classified, after prsesing "Ord": regularized variants (clicking in "show non-edition subreadings"), orthographic variants (in grey, if there is a non-expected orthographical variant) and variants (meaningful for the understanding of the text).

Critical Commentaries: we provide here, on one side, the divergences between the CAB edition and other editions (Geldner, Kellens, Pirart, Humbach…), together with some bibliography, if required. Furthermore, we offer some recitation instructions given in the manuscripts, such as the number of repetitions of one stanza, of a prayer and the ways the manuscripts abbreviate, such as čiyōn pad … nibišt ("as it is written in…"), etc.

Parallels: In this section, different type of parallels are to be seen:

  • quotations, if the stanza quotes or is quoted in another stanza. This is valid just for quotations of Old Avestan into Middle or Young Avestan.

  • Stanzas with the same text that are not necessarily quotations are displayed with "=";

  • structural parallels, this means, stanzas playing the same role in the cursus of the recitation (mostly in the litanies), are displayed with "≈"; other types of minor parallels (similar texts, similar sentences or similar syntagma) are displayed with "~".

Ritual Information: this section has several sub-sections:

  • alternative versions of the nērangs beside the one that is shown at the ceremony generator). The numbering corresponds to Cantera’s critical edition on the nērangs (in press)

  • quotations of the passage in the Pahlavi and Persian meta-ritual literature (such as the Nerangestān and the Revāyāt)

  • Bibliographical references regarding the "modern practice" (e.g. Anklesaria, Kotwal and Boyd, etc.), only if the modern practice differs from what it is attested in the manuscripts or the meta-ritual literature.

  • If there is any further remark, that is not present in any other bibliography, we add it at the end under "Commentaries".

Translation: The translation of the intended Stanza in modern European languages can be seen in this part.
